Ideal Jobs

Every job requires IT and technical knowledge and experience, however each job requires it at a different level. Johnny’s ideal job is more business related, so it requires a less extensive IT skill set in comparison to Mylaad’s and Phannavy’s jobs which requires an exceptional IT skill set with extensive experience. Otherwise all roles require IT knowledge and expertise. Some jobs require more client interaction than others, and drive with the purpose of client deliverables and satisfaction. Moreover, some jobs also require more project and team based work than others that are more individual work based. All of the jobs are looking for hard workers with great people skills, with the ability to communicate efficiently, work in teams, and lead and manage projects, teams, deliverables etc. Three years is the minimum years of experience required across all roles, with the exception of Phannavy’s requiring seven plus.

Group Reflection

As a group, we believed we did a good job working together to complete our assignment. We all communicated with each other effectively to ensure that we were on top of things. We allocated tasks to members from the start of the project to ensure that it is done efficiently, and so that each member can start on their activity and complete it simultaneously. We also completed our allocated tasks in time and we all put in a lot of effort to complete the workload. There were no implications or issues between group members and it was great to see us from different degrees and experiences coming together to provide different insights and knowledge. What worked well was that every group member put in effort into their work and showed that they had lots of prior knowledge and were also keen to learn more. Additionally, group members filled each other in if they missed anything and on their work progress. Definitely one of the biggest challenges we faced as a team was deciding on which project to undertake. However, through discussion via meetings, we were able to come to an idea that we all agreed on. Microsoft Teams was also a great tool to manage our communication, workflow and keeping everything organised. We were able to send our files on there, re-watch our meetings and read any missed conversations.

What could be improved was our team meetings. Some meetings could not be attended by team members due to conflicting schedules that were unavoidable such as work commitments and other classes. However, we worked around this by writing down notes on what each member had to do on Google Docs and letting the team member know what they missed out on via Microsoft Teams chat. Moreover, the meetings we did have allowed us to discuss our assignment in depth and any current challenges that we needed to address and overcome.

One surprising thing we learnt from working as a group was how each group member had their own unique working style. Some member’s strengths would accommodate another's weaknesses.

One thing we have learnt about group work was the importance of communication. Without communication and reminders we would not have been able to overcome challenges and issues that arose during the course of the project. Such examples were mentioned previously such as deciding which IT project to undertake. We all voiced our thoughts, ideas and concerns for each of our individual IT projects which ultimately led us to come to an agreement on our IT Project idea to bring to life. By having effective communication between our team members, we were able to build a strong team relationship and work together to finish our assigned tasks with confidence.
